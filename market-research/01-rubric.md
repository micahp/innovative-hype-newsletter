# Idea Evaluation Rubric

Weighted criteria for evaluating money-making ideas against Micah's constraints (solo operator, drives all day so needs 80–95% hands-off, has photogen.innovativehype.xyz running on Contabo VPS, Next.js skills, wants to solve the "find customers for my SaaS" catch-22).

| Criterion | Weight | What it measures | Scoring (1–5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hands-off ratio** | 25% | Can he run it while driving 8 hrs/day? | 5 = <5 hrs/week ongoing; 1 = daily computer time required |
| **Distribution solved** | 25% | Does the channel send him customers, vs. him chasing them? Breaks the SaaS catch-22. | 5 = platform/algorithm delivers traffic; 1 = pure cold outreach |
| **Time to first $** | 15% | Speed from launch to first dollar. | 5 = weeks; 1 = 6+ months |
| **Realistic ceiling** | 15% | Median operator outcome (not lottery winners). | 5 = $20K+/mo plausible; 1 = $500/mo cap |
| **Stack leverage** | 10% | Use of photogen, Next.js, VPS, AI image-gen skills. | 5 = direct fit; 1 = unrelated |
| **Upfront cost** | 5% | Cash + setup hours before launch. | 5 = weekend + <$100; 1 = months + $5K+ |
| **Defensibility** | 5% | Compounds vs. commodity race-to-zero. | 5 = network effects / owned audience; 1 = anyone can copy in a day |

## Why these weights

- **Hands-off and Distribution are the two non-negotiables** (50% combined). Either one failing kills the idea outright for Micah's situation.
- **Time to first $** matters because long ramps without revenue feedback get abandoned by solo operators.
- **Ceiling** matters but is penalized lower than the above because a small reliable income beats a hypothetical large one.
- **Stack leverage, cost, defensibility** are tiebreakers, not deciders.

## How to use

Score each idea 1–5 per criterion. Weighted sum produces a 1–5 total. Anything under 3.0 is filtered out before deeper evaluation.
