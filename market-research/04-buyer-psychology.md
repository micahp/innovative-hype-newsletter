# Buyer Psychology — what actually moves money

The foundational principles every persona section below assumes. If a product/funnel doesn't pull at least 3 of these, it won't convert.

## The core finding

**95% of purchasing decisions are made unconsciously, driven by emotion, then rationalized.** Logic is post-hoc. This is not opinion — it's neuromarketing consensus. Every successful info-product funnel exploits this. The pitch is emotional; the page after the buy button is logical.

## Cialdini's six (still works in 2026)

1. **Reciprocity** — give value first (free PDF, free 5-day email course), buyers feel obligated to return it. Foundation of every lead magnet.
2. **Commitment/consistency** — small yes leads to bigger yes. Newsletter signup → quiz → tripwire → main offer.
3. **Social proof** — "12,847 students enrolled." In 2026, trust in institutions is low but trust in peers is high.
4. **Authority** — credentials, books, podcast features, "as seen in."
5. **Liking** — parasocial bond (see women section); buyers buy from people they feel they know.
6. **Scarcity** — countdown timers, "only 47 spots." **Most powerful trigger of all** per 2026 research — outperforms personalization.

## 2026-specific buying triggers

- **FOMO marketing is core 2026 tactic** — limited drops, exclusive cohorts, "doors close Friday." Works because attention is the bottleneck.
- **Anchoring** — first price shapes everything. List $997, sell $297. Cross out the higher number visibly.
- **Loss aversion** — "Stop losing $X/month to [problem]" outperforms "Gain $X." 2:1 in conversion testing.
- **The dopamine loop** — research confirms purchasing literally releases dopamine. The shopping experience itself is the reward; this is why one-click buy and frictionless checkout matter.
- **Storytelling > features** — emotional narrative outperforms feature lists 5–10×. The story is "you, transformed."

## What makes someone buy an info product specifically

The buyer is not buying information (it's free on YouTube). They're buying:

1. **A shortcut** — "skip the 200 hours of trial and error"
2. **A shame escape** — "you don't have to admit you don't know this; just buy the course"
3. **An identity upgrade** — "become a person who [X]"
4. **A community** — proof they belong with people doing the thing
5. **Permission/accountability** — having paid creates the obligation to act

The pitch should hit #1 and #3 in the headline; #2, #4, #5 in the body.

## Pricing psychology

- **Price ladder always**: free → $27 → $97 → $497 → $1,997. Each tier qualifies the buyer for the next.
- **Charm pricing** ($97 vs $100) lifts conversion ~5–10% at low ticket; **prestige pricing** ($1,000 over $997) works for high-ticket.
- **Three-option pricing** with middle option highlighted → 60%+ pick middle (decoy effect).
- **Payment plans** (3× $97 vs $297) raise conversion on courses ~30%.

## The 2026 trust shift

> "Trust in institutions is low. Trust in peers and parasocial figures is high."

This is the single most important sentence in this entire research dump. It means:
- Brand-led marketing fails
- Founder-led / faceless creator-led wins
- Reviews and creator endorsements > paid ads
- A no-name TikToker can outsell a major brand
