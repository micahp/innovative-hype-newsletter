Here's how to get $20+ RPM on 1 Hour+ long gaming YouTube videos.

Before is how YouTube used to work.

After is how it works now.

Gone are the days of - " Today I'm playing this game and hopefully I can do X"

Your videos need a clear end goal. A clear mission, a north star, that everything in the rest of the video feeds into and works towards.

Your super long videos need a backbone. A reason for the viewer to commit to watching all the way to the end.

Your videos need ups and downs. It can't just be mindlessly progressing in various directions.

Your videos need payoffs that keep it fresh and interesting. Your audience wants to be entertained, impressed, shocked consistently, not just at the very end.

Your video needs to get progressively more interesting/challenging. In most games, that'll happen naturally, but what reason does a viewer have to keep watching a video that stays the same all the way through. 

Your video needs foreshadowing. Why should I keep watching? What can I look forward to? What's around the corner?
